# Social Media Playbook

> Brand voice, engagement strategy, and posting guidelines for LinkedIn and X (Twitter)

---

## Brand Identity

**Role:** Technical Advisor (NOT freelancer, consultant, or developer for hire)

**Tagline:** "I help CTOs avoid $500K mistakes"

**Positioning:** Strategic architecture guidance for ambitious startups

---

## Voice Characteristics

### 1. Technical Precision

- Always use specific numbers: 40%, 10x, 100k+, $315-595K
- Never vague: "A full-time CTO costs $315-595K in year one" not "CTOs are expensive"

### 2. Direct & Authoritative

- Take clear positions, no hedging
- "Your startup doesn't need Kubernetes" not "You might want to consider alternatives"

### 3. Business Context First

- Every technical point ties to business outcomes (revenue, time, cost, risk)
- "Stop optimizing for hourly rate. Start optimizing for cost per unit of delivered value."

### 4. Experienced Perspective

- Speak from lived experience, not theory
- "I've advised 30+ startups..." / "The founders I work with..." / "I've seen the failure patterns..."

### 5. Dense Information

- Every sentence carries weight, no filler
- Avoid: "In today's world", "It goes without saying", "As we all know"

### 6. Contrarian When Warranted

- Challenge conventional wisdom with evidence
- "A $200/hr senior developer often costs LESS than a $30/hr junior"

## LinkedIn Strategy

### Post Structure (Text Posts)

```
[Hook - contrarian or surprising statement]

[The math/evidence - specific numbers]

[What most people get wrong]

[What actually works - framework or insight]

[Personal experience tie-in]

[Closing question - specific, not "thoughts?"]
```

### Optimal Format

- **Length:** 1,000-1,300 characters (dwell time sweet spot)
- **Paragraphs:** Short, lots of white space
- **Line breaks:** Between every 1-2 sentences
- **Arrows/bullets:** Use sparingly for lists
- **Hashtags:** None (LinkedIn algorithm no longer boosts them in 2026 — uses keyword detection)
- **Links:** NEVER in post body (kills reach) — add in first comment if needed

### Best Posting Times

- Weekdays 8-10 AM EST
- Weekdays 5-7 PM EST
- Tuesday-Thursday perform best

### Engagement Priority (Comments)

**Always respond to:**

- Substantive counterpoints (shows you can handle pushback)
- People adding their own experience (builds community)
- Direct questions
- Other CTOs/founders (peer credibility)

**Brief acknowledgment only:**

- Simple agreement ("Great point!")
- One-line validations

**Skip:**

- Generic pitches disguised as comments
- Tangential AI/tech debates
- Hostile bad-faith comments
- Listicles/educational posts (not discussion starters)

### Response Tone

**When someone agrees:**

> Brief validation, maybe add one insight. Don't over-explain.

**When someone pushes back (valid point):**

> "Fair point. [Concede the specific thing]. The core argument is [restate thesis differently]."

**When someone pushes back (misread your post):**

> "Good question. [Clarify without being defensive]. The post is about [X], not [Y]."

**When someone asks a question:**

> Answer directly, then tie back to the broader point if relevant.

**When someone adds their experience:**

> Acknowledge their story, connect it to the pattern you're describing.

## X (Twitter) Strategy

### The Reality for New Accounts

| Activity                   | Time Split (0-500 followers) |
| -------------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| Engaging (replies, quotes) | 70-80%                       |
| Posting original content   | 20-30%                       |

**Posting into a void doesn't work.** The algorithm won't show your content without engagement history.

### What Actually Works

1. **Join X Communities**
   - "Build in Public" (180K+ members)
   - Tech/Startup communities
   - Posts in Communities appear in dedicated feed all members see

2. **Reply Strategy**
   - Thoughtful replies on larger accounts' posts
   - Add value, don't just agree
   - Your profile gets seen by their audience

3. **Threads > Single Tweets**
   - 3x more engagement
   - Shows expertise
   - 8-12 tweets optimal

4. **Post Frequency**
   - 1-3 quality posts/day when starting
   - Replies count toward activity

### X Post Format

**Single Post:**

```
[Contrarian hook]

[Evidence/experience in 1-2 sentences]

[What to do instead]
```

- Max 280 characters (Premium: up to 25,000)
- No hashtags (no algorithm boost in 2026, triggers spam filters at 5+)

**Thread:**

```
Tweet 1: Standalone hook (no "Thread:" or "1/")
Tweets 2-7: One insight each, concrete examples
Tweet 8: Summary + link (if applicable)
```

### Who to Engage With

**High-value accounts aligned with our positioning:**

- CTOs/engineering leaders sharing architecture decisions
- Founders discussing infrastructure trade-offs
- People posting about "boring technology", monolith vs microservices, premature optimization

**Skip:**

- Certification/bootcamp content
- Generic career advice listicles
- Obvious product promotions disguised as posts

## Reply Templates

### Agreeing While Adding Value

> "[Specific part] is the key insight. [Add one angle they didn't cover]. [Optional: tie to your experience]."

### Respectful Disagreement

> "Interesting take. I've seen the opposite pattern in [context] — [your observation]. Could be [reason for difference]."

### Answering a Question

> "[Direct answer]. The nuance is [caveat]. [Optional: reframe to broader principle]."

### War Story Response

> "[Validate their story]. Seen the same pattern — [your parallel]. The common thread is [pattern]."

### Conceding a Point

> "Fair — [specific thing] was too strong. [Refined position]. The core point stands: [thesis]."

## Engagement Workflow

### Daily (15-20 min)

1. Check notifications on LinkedIn and X
2. Respond to comments on your posts (priority: substantive ones)
3. Find 3-5 posts to engage with (replies that add value)

### Weekly

1. Review scheduled posts in Postiz
2. Draft 1-2 manual posts for gaps in schedule
3. Check X Communities for relevant discussions
4. Follow 5-10 relevant accounts

### Monthly

1. Review which posts performed best
2. Identify patterns (topics, formats, times)
3. Adjust content calendar based on data

## Red Lines

**Never do:**

- Engage with trolls or bad-faith comments
- Post the same content to LinkedIn and X verbatim (platform-native formats differ)
- Comment on competitor's posts promoting similar services
- Respond to every comment (let some threads resolve naturally)
- Use engagement bait ("Like if you agree!", "What do you think?")
- Post external links in LinkedIn post body

**Always do:**

- Respond to substantive pushback (shows confidence)
- Credit others when referencing their ideas
- Tie technical points to business outcomes
- Use specific numbers over vague claims
- Let good threads end naturally (don't over-engage)

## Measuring Success

### LinkedIn Metrics

- **Impressions:** Are posts getting seen?
- **Engagement rate:** Comments > reactions > shares
- **Profile views:** Are posts driving curiosity?
- **Connection requests:** Quality > quantity (CTOs, founders, eng leaders)

### X Metrics

- **Follower growth:** Slow and steady from 0
- **Reply engagement:** Are people responding to your replies?
- **Profile clicks:** From replies to profile visits

### Leading Indicators

- Comments from target audience (CTOs, founders, eng leaders)
- DMs asking about advisory services
- Mentions/tags in others' posts
- Reposts by accounts with larger followings

## 1. Backlink Building Services (White-Hat)

### 1A. Journalist Outreach / Expert Quote Platforms

The old HARO model has fragmented into several platforms. These are the highest-ROI options because a single journalist placement on a DR70+ publication can be worth $500-$1,500 in link value.

| Platform                                    | Model                                                                                                 | Cost                                                | Notes                                                                                                  |
| ------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Featured.com** (acquired HARO April 2025) | Email digests + dashboard. You respond to journalist queries, they select expert quotes for articles. | Free tier available; paid plans for priority access | Now owns the HARO brand. Relaunched the original email-based model. Free for sources and journalists.  |
| **Qwoted**                                  | Verified expert matching with journalists in real-time                                                | Free + paid tiers                                   | Verification requirement filters out low-quality pitchers. Higher conversion rates than HARO ever had. |
| **Source of Sources**                       | Media matching platform (same founder as original HARO -- Peter Shankman)                             | Free + paid                                         | Spiritual successor to HARO by the original creator.                                                   |
| **#JournoRequest on X**                     | Hashtag monitoring -- journalists post queries directly                                               | Free (time investment only)                         | Direct interaction with editors. Good for networking. Requires monitoring or automation.               |
| **Help a B2B Writer**                       | B2B-specific journalist outreach                                                                      | Free                                                | Smaller but highly targeted for B2B tech content.                                                      |

**Recommendation for alexmayhew.dev:** Start with Featured.com (free tier) and #JournoRequest on X. These cost nothing but time. As a technical advisor, CTO-level expertise is exactly what tech journalists want for quotes. Respond to 5-10 queries per week. Expected hit rate: 5-15% of pitches get placed.

## 6. What to AVOID

### Definite Scams / Will Hurt Rankings

| Red Flag                                               | Why It's Bad                                                                                                                                                                             |
| ------------------------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **PBN (Private Blog Network) links**                   | Self-referencing networks violating Google guidelines. Once discovered, severe penalties or deindexing. Sellers use terms like "in-house blogging network" or "privately managed sites." |
| **Fiverr backlink gigs** ($5-$50 for "1000 backlinks") | Come from PBNs, spammy forums, blog comments, and automated tools. Will trigger Google penalties.                                                                                        |
| **"Guaranteed #1 rankings"**                           | No legitimate SEO provider can guarantee rankings. If they promise this, they're lying.                                                                                                  |
| **"SEO blasts" (5,000+ links for $5-$50)**             | Automated spam. Will get you penalized.                                                                                                                                                  |
| **High DR/DA links with no traffic**                   | Red flag for link farms. A DR60 site with 0 organic traffic is almost certainly a PBN.                                                                                                   |
| **Extremely cheap niche edits ($15-$30 for DR40+)**    | Economics don't work for legitimate outreach. These are automated CMS insertions without editorial involvement.                                                                          |
| **Bulk directory submissions**                         | Outdated tactic. Most directories are low-quality or deindexed.                                                                                                                          |
| **Link exchanges / reciprocal link schemes**           | Google explicitly penalizes "excessive link exchanges."                                                                                                                                  |
| **Comment spam / forum profile links**                 | Automated tools. Zero value, potential penalty.                                                                                                                                          |
| **Services with no transparency about target sites**   | If they won't tell you where links will be placed BEFORE you pay, they're hiding something.                                                                                              |

### Warning Signs in Vendor Communication

- "We have relationships with 500+ websites" -- likely means overused sites that have lost credibility
- "Guaranteed placements in 48 hours" -- real editorial outreach takes weeks
- Complicated jargon about "footprints" -- PBN sellers trying to sound credible
- "White-hat" used excessively without explaining methodology
- No case studies or verifiable client references
- Contracts that lock you in for 12+ months upfront

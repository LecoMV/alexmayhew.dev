## Responding to Questions

### Direct technical question:

**Comment:**

> "Shouldn't smoke testing, CI/CD, QA, etc. be catching some of these bugs before they reach production anyway?"

**Reply:**

> CI/CD catches what you write tests for. The senior advantage is avoiding entire categories of bugs that never get written in the first place. No test catches an architecture decision that creates a scaling bottleneck 12 months later.

**Why it works:** Answers directly, then pivots to the deeper insight (architecture > code quality).

---

## What NOT to Reply To

**Comment:**

> "Why would we read your post when you couldn't bother to write it? AI-posting is not making you look visionary."

**Action:** Ignore completely. The post has 20+ substantive comments from senior engineers. That's your answer.

---

### Tangential debates:

**Comment:**

> "The cost of AI writing the code with all of its mistakes will cost even more."

**Action:** Skip. It's a one-line hot take about AI that pulls the thread away from your argument.

**Action:** Skip. They're making your same argument. Engaging boosts their post when yours is already live.
